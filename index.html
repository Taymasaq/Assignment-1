The World’s Greatest Detective

Sherlock Holmes, a fictional detective created by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle in 1887, is one of the most recognized literary characters in history. Known for his sharp intellect, deductive reasoning, and eccentric habits, Holmes has inspired countless adaptations in literature, film, and television.

Early Life and Residence

While Conan Doyle never revealed much about Holmes’s childhood, the detective is famously based at 221B Baker Street in London, UK. He shares his residence with Dr. John Watson, his friend and biographer. Together, they investigate the city’s most complex crimes during the late Victorian and Edwardian eras.

For background on Doyle, see the Encyclopaedia Britannica entry. You can also read many Holmes stories for free on Project Gutenberg.

Traits and Legacy

Core Abilities

  • Master of deductive reasoning
  • Skilled in disguise and deception
  • Deep knowledge of chemistry, tobacco ash, and violin

Why Holmes Endures

Holmes represents the triumph of logic over chaos. His partnership with Watson models collaboration and trust, and his methods continue to influence modern forensic science.

As Holmes says, When you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth.
